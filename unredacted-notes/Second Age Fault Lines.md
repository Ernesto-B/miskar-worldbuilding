# I. Second Age Ideological Fault Lines

### What the War of Night “Proved” (According to Different Peoples)
The Second Age is not defined by peace.  
It is defined by **interpretation**.

Everyone lived through the same catastrophe.  
Almost no one agrees on what it meant.

These disagreements form **belief blocs**, not nations. Borders will later harden around them.

---

## 1. The Absolutists
**Core Belief:**  
“The world nearly ended because mortals overreached.”

**Interpretation of the War of Night:**
- Magic saved Miskar, yes.
- But only because the gods _allowed it_.
- The Grey rose because people forgot their place.

**View of Magic:**
- Necessary.
- Dangerous.
- Must be regulated by divine authority.

**Who gravitates here:**
- The Celestial Order’s upper ranks
- Many human kingdoms
- Some conservative elven enclaves
- Urban populations that survived intact

**Second Age Behavior:**
- Push licensing, doctrine, obedience.
- Rewrite history to emphasize divine mercy.
- Frame dissent as existential risk.

**Narrative Use:**  
This bloc creates _stability through repression_.  
They are not wrong, but they are incomplete.

---

## 2. The Preservationists
**Core Belief:**  
“The world survived because knowledge endured.”

**Interpretation of the War of Night:**
- Victory came from preparation, memory, and sacrifice.
- The Celestial Order did not win the war.
- People did.

**View of Magic:**
- A tool with a cost.
- Safer when studied, recorded, and contextualized.
- Dangerous when mythologized.

**Who gravitates here:**
- Elves
- Ancient halfling networks
- Some dwarven scholars
- Quiet institutions, not loud ones

**Second Age Behavior:**
- Hoard records.
- Trade favors, not allegiance.
- Preserve truths quietly rather than confront authority directly.

**Narrative Use:**  
This bloc knows the world is lying to itself and survives by _outlasting_ the lie.

---

## 3. The Pragmatists
**Core Belief:**  
“The world almost ended because no one adapted fast enough.”

**Interpretation of the War of Night:**
- Tradition failed.
- Doctrine slowed response.
- Innovation saved lives.

**View of Magic:**
- Dangerous, yes.
- But everything is dangerous.
- Better to use it well than fear it.

**Who gravitates here:**
- Tiefling communities
- Border kingdoms
- Desert cultures
- Artificers and mercantile powers

**Second Age Behavior:**
- Invest in technology, relics, expeditions.
- Ignore doctrine when survival demands it.
- Exploit ambiguity.

**Narrative Use:**  
This bloc produces progress _and_ catastrophe.

---

## 4. The Withdrawalists
**Core Belief:**  
“The world was scarred because it was too interconnected.”

**Interpretation of the War of Night:**
- Large systems attract large disasters.
- Centralization magnifies collapse.

**View of Magic:**
- Too loud.
- Too visible.
- Too attractive to forces beyond control.

**Who gravitates here:**
- Wood elves
- Some goliath clans
- Remote communities
- Scarred regions that survived alone

**Second Age Behavior:**
- Disperse populations.
- Reject empires.
- Avoid being important.

**Narrative Use:**  
These are the people who survive when everything else burns again.

---

## 5. The Silent Dissenters
**Core Belief:**  
“Something about the War of Night was wrong.”

**Interpretation of the War of Night:**
- Too many contradictions.
- Too many erased names.
- Too clean an ending for something that broke the world.

**View of Magic:**
- A symptom, not the cause.

**Who gravitates here:**
- Dragonborn
- Dimmed peoples
- Certain scholars, exiles, and veterans

**Second Age Behavior:**
- Ask forbidden questions.
- Avoid open alignment.
- Watch.

**Narrative Use:**  
This bloc generates your deepest revelations.

# II. The Celestial Order

### What Is Known, Believed, and Enforced
The Order is not monolithic.  
Its power comes from **layered ignorance**.

---

## 1. The Celestial Crown
**What the Crown Knows:**
- The Seal exists.
- Magic weakens it.
- Total failure would be catastrophic.
- Thaurûn’s nature is not what doctrine claims.

**What the Crown Believes:**
- Control is preferable to truth.
- The world cannot survive full revelation.
- The Order must endure, even if faith erodes.

**Burden:**  
The Crown governs while knowing the system is unsustainable.

---

## 2. The High Cardinals (No More Than Three)
**What They Know:**
- Magic has a cost.
- The Seal is fragile.
- The First Age narrative is incomplete.

**What They Do Not Know (or Deny):**
- The full implications of Thaurûn’s nature.
- The depth of Old God influence.

**Role:**  
They translate divine anxiety into policy.

---

## 3. The Greater Clergy
**What They Believe:**
- Magic is dangerous because doctrine says so.
- Licensing preserves order.
- Disobedience invites catastrophe.

**What They Enforce:**
- Registration. 
- Oversight.
- Public rituals reinforcing fear and gratitude.

They do not ask _why_.

---

## 4. The Lower Clergy
**What They Experience:**
- Contradictions.
- Suffering.
- The quiet failure of promises.

**What They Do:**
- Enforce rules they do not fully believe.
- Rationalize inconsistencies.
- Sometimes look away.

This is where schisms begin.

# III. Second Age Map Logic

### Pressure Zones, Not Borders
Before you draw borders, identify **pressure**.
See [[Miskar Lands]] for more details

---

## 1. Scarred Lands Pressure
- Grey-blighted zones
- Ashfields
- Cratered plains

**Who wants them:**
- Pragmatists for resources
- Preservationists for knowledge
- The Order to quarantine them

---

## 2. Knowledge Pressure
- Ancient forests
- Subterranean ruins
- Island labyrinths

**Conflict:**  
Who controls memory controls narrative.

---

## 3. Faith Pressure
- Temperate heartlands
- The mountain city above the clouds

**Conflict:**  
Visibility versus authority.

---

## 4. Survival Pressure
- Frost lands
- Verdant False Lands
- Coastal regions

**Conflict:**  
Short-term survival versus long-term collapse.

---

## 5. Untethered Zones
- Remote goliath ranges
- Wood elf territories
- Dragonborn enclaves

These regions resist maps entirely.
